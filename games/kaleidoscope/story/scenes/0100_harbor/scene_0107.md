# Scene: scene_0107

## Title
The Fishermen's Argument

## Location
Harbor Market

---

## Scene Text

Two fishermen stand beside a pile of tangled nets, arguing loudly enough that half the market can hear them.

"I’m telling you the currents have changed," one insists, jabbing a finger toward the sea. "Fish don’t vanish like that for no reason."

The other shakes his head stubbornly.

"Currents don’t move that fast. Something’s driving them deeper. Something big."

Several nearby merchants pretend not to listen while clearly listening very carefully.

One of the fishermen notices you watching and gives a short laugh.

"Traveler, you spend enough time around docks and you'll hear ten different explanations for the same problem."

He gestures toward the harbor waters.

"But something strange has been happening out there lately. Nets come back empty. Boats report odd lights on the water some nights."

The other fisherman crosses his arms.

"And that's not even the strangest thing folks have seen around this island."

The market noise swells around you as their argument slowly fades back into the background chatter of the harbor.
