# Scene: scene_0106

## Title
The Compass Merchant

## Location
Harbor Market

---

## Scene Text

The woman polishing the brass compass looks up as you approach.

Her stall is small but carefully arranged. Several instruments rest on a dark cloth: compasses, small measuring tools, and a few curious devices you don't immediately recognize.

She finishes polishing the compass and holds it up so the needle catches the light.

"A good compass is worth more than most travelers realize," she says. "Especially on an island like this one."

She turns the instrument slightly. The needle settles, pointing steadily north.

"Places like Harbor Island attract curious people," she continues. "Explorers. Scholars. Dreamers."

Her eyes flick briefly toward the hill where the library stands.

"And sometimes people looking for things they don't quite understand."

She sets the compass gently back on the table.

"If you're planning to explore the island, a sense of direction can be very useful."

Around you, the market continues its noisy rhythm of trade and conversation.
