# Scene: scene_1401

## Title
The Inland Station

## Location
Inland Ridge Path / Cleared Survey Ground

---

## Scene Text

Past the second marked platform, the path narrows and rises into denser country. The symbol line points deeper, but here the path shows signs of something else: deliberate clearing.

A small level ground has been opened on the ridge, where undergrowth has been cut back and stone arranged to form a low platform. The platform sits in a position that commands view across the inland country in three directions.

Near the platform edge, charcoal fragments lie scattered and sorted — the residue of rubbings taken from stone. Paper scraps, darkened and stiffened by age and weather but still legible, sit weighted under stones preserved from rain. Fragments of what were once careful notes, water-stained and incomplete, describe specific marks, measurements, distances.

The handwriting matches the journals from Harbor Island library.

This is where someone stayed. Not to rest, but to study.

Someone who came here multiple times, who brought materials, who documented what they found.

The station faces the continuing symbol line.

The work was methodical. The investigator did not rush.
