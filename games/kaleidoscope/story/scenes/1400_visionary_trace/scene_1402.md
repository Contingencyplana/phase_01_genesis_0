## Title
The Visionary's Field Notes

## Location
Inland Ridge Path / Study Station Archive

## Scene Text

Beneath a stone shelter built into the ridge face, protected from direct rain but open to air and cold, a small collection of materials lies preserved in layers.

Maps. Sketch pages. Dated entries written in the same precise hand as the Harbor Island journals.

The maps show this exact inland region in detail, with careful notations marking each circle-and-dot site. Numbers recording distances between marks. Symbols indicating direction of travel and observation points. Annotations comparing findings to the archive sheets from Tidemarket and Ledger House.

The Visionary studied the same contradiction the Guide explained at the archives: records that disagreed with each other because they were all right, describing the same working ground from different institutional perspectives. The Visionary understood what the civic machinery was still debating.

Dated field entries chronicle specific visits:

*"Third site confirms the spacing matches the relay unit. The marks are not random."*

*"The line does not terminate. It continues as the oldest carrier route suggests."*

*"Four sites visited. The arrangement is deliberate. Someone marked this path to be found by someone traveling prepared to see the marks."*

The final visible entry is marked two seasons prior.

*"Moving forward along the continuation. The destination may finally be within reach."*

After that, the record stops.

The maps and notes show the player's own current route — the specific path they are following — already traced and documented in the Visionary's hand.

This is not coincidence. This is inheritance.
