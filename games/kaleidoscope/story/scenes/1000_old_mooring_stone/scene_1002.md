# Scene: scene_1002

## Title
At the Rope Line

## Location
Edge of Tidemarket / Roped Quay Section

---

## Scene Text

The roped section has become a public knot of voices, chalk marks, and measuring twine.

A dockworker squats by the planks and drags a line from bollard to bollard, showing where two new berths would run if the stone were moved inland by a body length.

"That buys turn space," he says. "Two hulls clear instead of one."

An older local folds her arms and answers, "You gain room and lose memory. That stone has held this edge longer than half this harbor has stood."

A launch captain cuts in with flat urgency.

"I don't care what we call it if my passengers miss the evening transfer again. Work around it, move it, just decide."

Someone from the wharf office writes numbers on a slate: berth angle, queue depth, estimated delay per tide window.

Nothing here is abstract.

Geometry, schedule, and caution are all being argued in the same breath.

When one worker suggests prying the stone free before dawn, three others object at once.

"No moving until it's read," someone says.

"Read by who?"

A runner points toward the wall where a survey kit has been set down beside wrapped charcoal sheets.

The debate shifts from whether the stone matters to how anyone can prove what it says.
