# Scene: scene_1003

## Title
Survey at Low Spray

## Location
Edge of Tidemarket / Stone Face and Retaining Wall

---

## Scene Text

A survey hand kneels beside the mooring stone while two others hold a canvas screen against the wind.

He dusts charcoal lightly across a thin sheet pressed to the weathered face, then peels it back and studies the broken lines that appear.

"Not clean enough," he says. "Too much salt wear."

He tries again with a different angle, tracing shallow grooves with a bone stylus and calling out partial forms to a clerk.

"Curve here. Cut there. Maybe a tie mark. Maybe not."

A younger worker watches and mutters, "So we stop work for maybe-not?"

The survey hand does not look up.

"You stop work for certainty you don't have yet. That's different."

No one finds a full reading.

What emerges is only enough to harden disagreement:

some hear old designation,
some hear superstition,
some hear delay dressed as reverence.

The stone remains ambiguous, but no longer dismissible.

The marks may mean something practical, ceremonial, both, or neither.

No one can prove it today.
