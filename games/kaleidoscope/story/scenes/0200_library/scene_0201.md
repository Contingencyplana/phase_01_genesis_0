# Scene: scene_0201

## Title
Inside the Island Library

## Location
Island Library

---

## Scene Text

You push the heavy wooden door open and step inside the island library.

The air smells faintly of dust, old paper, and sea wind drifting through tall windows that overlook the harbor. Long wooden shelves stretch across the room, filled with weathered journals, rolled maps, and carefully stacked bundles of handwritten notes.

A large table stands in the center of the room. Several open books lie across its surface as if someone had been studying them not long ago.

Most of the journals appear to describe ordinary things — shipping routes, weather patterns, records of fishing seasons — but a few stand apart. Their pages are filled with sketches of unusual devices, strange diagrams, and long passages of careful writing.

One open journal catches your eye.

The handwriting is precise and deliberate.

The first page reads:

*"If these ideas are correct, then the island is not merely a harbor. It is a beginning."*

You glance around the quiet room.

Whoever wrote these journals believed something important was hidden here.

Outside, the harbor continues its quiet rhythm far below the hill.

Inside the library, the silence feels different — almost as if the room itself is waiting.
