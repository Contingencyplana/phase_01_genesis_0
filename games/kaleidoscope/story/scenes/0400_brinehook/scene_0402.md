## Scene Text

The captain brings the sailboat alongside a weathered timber wharf darkened by years of salt and rain.

A pair of small boats are tied nearby, their hulls patched and practical. Nets hang from pegs. Rope coils sit beside fish crates stamped with old supply marks.

The air smells of kelp, brine, and wood smoke from somewhere uphill.

Voices carry from the cottages, not loud, just ordinary: someone calling for a bucket, someone else laughing, a door closing against the wind.

Brinehook feels quieter than Harbor Island.

Smaller, closer, and lived in every day.
