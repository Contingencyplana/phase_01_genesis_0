# Scene: scene_0404

## Title
The Captain's Family

## Location
Cottage Yard

---

## Scene Text

The captain's daughter sets the basket down and wipes her hands on her apron.

"Most weeks are simple," she says. "Fish when the water gives us fish. Repairs when it doesn't."

She glances toward the wharf where her father checks the sail lines again.

"We trade with Harbor Island for flour, lamp oil, and nails. Send back what we can spare. If weather turns, everyone waits. If weather clears, everyone moves."

A gull lands on the fish rack and is chased off with practiced impatience.

She half-smiles.

"Islands look separate on a map. Out here, they're family if they want to keep going."

The words are plain, but they carry the weight of habit and years.
