## Title
The Low Rise

## Location

## Scene Text

A short climb brings you to a low grassy rise above the wharf.

From here, Brinehook Island looks compact and practical: cottages gathered against the wind, workyards close to shore, boats pulled where they can be reached quickly.

Across the channel, Harbor Island sits on the horizon, familiar even at this distance. Its hill and treeline are softened by sea light, but its shape is unmistakable.

The crossing suddenly feels measurable.

Not legend. Not rumor.

Just islands linked by weather, wood, and people willing to sail between them.

Near the edge of the rise, a weathered object stands half-sunk beside the path.
