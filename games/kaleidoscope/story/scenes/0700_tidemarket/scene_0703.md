## Scene Text

The market quarter is denser than anything you've seen so far in the archipelago.

Stalls press close under patched canvas. People bargain in quick bursts, then break off to chase a better queue before coming back to finish the argument.

You pass baskets of dried kelp, lamp wicks, hooks, salt cakes, smoked fish, sailcloth strips, and jars of ground spice stacked in careful pyramids.

At one corner stall, a woman with bright sleeves and a sharper voice is measuring spice into folded paper while talking over three customers at once.

"No, the clove mix is short because the morning run came in light," she says. "Take half now or wait and complain later with everyone else."

A man in line answers, "If this lane keeps stalling, we'll all be eating boiled root by week's end."

Nobody laughs.

The market spice seller finishes a bundle, slides it across the board, and says, "Delays travel farther than boats. That's the rule."

Around you, practical frustration and rumor move together like tide and chop.
