# Scene: scene_0701

## Title
Arrival at Tidemarket

## Location
Tidemarket Harborfront

---

## Scene Text

The public ferry noses into Tidemarket under shouted signals and bell taps from the quay.

By the time the plank drops, three crews are already unloading crates, two passengers are arguing over queue order, and a runner is weaving through the crowd with tied notices in hand.

Harbor Island felt busy when you first arrived there.

Tidemarket feels bigger in a different way: less like one village working at one pace, more like many needs colliding at once.

Voices overlap in layers.

Cargo calls. Route calls. Price calls. Delay calls.

A woman near the rail mutters, "If the noon channel slips again, half the outer run misses the light."

Nobody stops moving long enough to answer her.

Ahead, the harborfront opens into lanes of barrels, rope, wet boards, and waiting people trying to decide where to stand without blocking someone else's work.
