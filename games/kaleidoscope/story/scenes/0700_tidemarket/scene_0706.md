# Scene: scene_0706

## Title
Gathering Lane

## Location
Tidemarket Gathering Lane

---

## Scene Text

Gathering Lane runs between market sheds and a low wall above the water.

People wait here when they are early, delayed, uncertain, or watching for someone who should have arrived already.

A messenger leans against a post with sealed packets tucked under one arm.

Two net-menders argue quietly about berth assignments.

A family from an outer island counts bundles twice, then a third time.

A pair of workers compare route rumors with the seriousness of people discussing weather before a long crossing.

No one owns the lane, but everyone uses it.

This is where notice, gossip, complaint, and practical advice braid together into shared public memory.

You overhear a phrase repeated in different mouths: inner channels tightening.

Then another, lower and less certain: old places should have been left alone.

Tidemarket feels fully social here, not just commercial.
