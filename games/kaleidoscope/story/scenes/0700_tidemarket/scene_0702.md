# Scene: scene_0702

## Title
The Harborfront

## Location
Tidemarket Harborfront

---

## Scene Text

The harborfront runs on organized disorder.

Deckhands pass sacks in a human chain while porters shoulder baskets toward painted lane marks that are already half covered in mud and fish scales.

A board of routes hangs beside the wharf office, but most people seem to follow shouted updates instead.

"Inner lane delayed!"

"West buoy run loading now!"

"Keep the plank clear!"

At a narrow standing desk near the office door, a tired clerk is taking names, stamping slips, and fielding complaints without looking up for more than a second at a time.

You catch someone call him the Wharf Clerk.

He points two workers left, waves one courier forward, then snaps to a traveler behind you, "If you're asking where your slot went, read the board first, then come back."

The place is rough at the edges, but nothing here is accidental.

Too many crossings depend on this quay for anything to be casual.
