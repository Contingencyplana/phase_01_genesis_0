# Scene: scene_1302

## Title
The Rubbing Sheets

## Location
Inland Archives / Comparison Table

---

## Scene Text

Back at the comparison table, the charcoal rubbing taken from the inland shelf lies flat beside the older archive sheets.

The rubbings already on file — copies of marks from the forest stone circle, the Brinehook lookout marker, the Lantern Key foundation stone, the resting stone at Saint Elmo's Rest — were gathered as curiosities and filed without interpretation.

Side by side, they are harder to treat as curiosities.

The marks do not vary in meaning. They vary only in weathering, in depth of cut, and in the type of stone they were taken from. The form is consistent across sites that do not share geography, function, or age of construction.

The Guide examines the new rubbing without surprise.

"There will be more," he says. "Wherever someone needed to mark a place they expected to find again from a direction they hadn't arrived from yet."

He does not explain it further. He does not have to.

The pattern is now visible. What remains is confirmation.
