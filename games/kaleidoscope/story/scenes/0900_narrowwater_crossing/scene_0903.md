# Scene: scene_0903

## Title
Window Release at the Flag Line

## Location
Narrowwater Crossing / Signal and Assignment Point

---

## Scene Text

A bell taps twice from the post, and the staging noise tightens into attention.

The signal hand changes two channel flags and waits for the Marshal's nod before raising the third.

"Inner cut open for shallow draft only," the Marshal calls. "Outer line restricted. Drift reported near the west bend."

He points in sequence with the brass tube.

"Courier skiff first. Fish barge second if she clears the marker without scraping. Passenger launch deferred to next window. Heavy cargo waits."

A groan rises from the launch and is swallowed by the wind.

"You can file a contest at intake," the Marshal says without heat. "You cannot argue depth with stone."

Two crew chiefs trade hard looks, then start issuing practical orders instead of objections.

Ropes come loose.

The courier slides forward.

The barge follows slower, stern drifting before the helm catches the turn.

Behind them, the deferred boats hold position and recalculate what the next delay will cost.

Coordination here is not fairness in the abstract.

It is triage under tide, weather, and channel geometry.
