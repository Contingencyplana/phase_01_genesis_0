# Scene: scene_0904

## Title
Spillover After the Deferred Window

## Location
Narrowwater Crossing / Return Flow Toward Tidemarket

---

## Scene Text

By the time the released boats clear the bend, the deferred crews are already turning delay into paperwork, warnings, and hard choices.

A runner copies lane marks from a damp slate and heads for the route board before the ink can smear.

One trader starts dictating a loss note to a mate: missed transfer, chilled hold warming, sequence contested.

A passenger crew decides to split loads and send only urgent parcels on the next available skiff.

Nobody calls this dramatic.

They call it Tuesday tide.

Still, the pattern is plain.

One local deferral has already become three civic consequences: a public notice revision, an intake dispute, and a market shortfall by evening.

Narrowwater does not end at the channel mouth.

It continues through the systems that absorb delay.
