# Scene: scene_0902

## Title
Queue at the Narrowwater Staging Line

## Location
Narrowwater Crossing / Staging Buoys

---

## Scene Text

Below the overlook, the staging line is a crowded braid of hulls, ropes, and waiting faces.

A fish barge rides low and heavy, crew already eyeing the sun as if it might charge them rent.

A passenger launch sits two lengths behind it, crowded with travelers holding bundles and trying not to show they are counting the minutes.

A narrow courier skiff keeps edging forward, then backing off when the signal hand points with a chalked paddle toward the marked wait line.

No one here is idle.

Crews coil and uncoil lines. Porters retie loads. A mother keeps one hand on a child's shoulder while watching the channel flags change from one pattern to another.

You hear the same question in three voices: "This window or the next?"

When the tide shifts, waiting becomes risk.

Spoilage, missed transfers, and lost light all begin here, before any official dispute is written down.

The Ferry Marshal steps onto a low bollard and calls for lane marks, cargo type, and draft depth, one vessel at a time.

The bottleneck is not hidden at this distance.

It is procedural, public, and strained.
