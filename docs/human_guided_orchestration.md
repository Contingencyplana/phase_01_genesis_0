# Human-Guided Orchestration

## Purpose

Human-Guided Orchestration describes the working method currently used to build the Interactive Novel and the broader `phase_01_genesis_0` workspace.

It exists to preserve direction, reduce drift, and keep the project playable and survivable as it grows.

This file is not a technical specification for every future automation layer.

It is a compact statement of how orchestration currently works.

---

## Core Principle

The project is human-guided.

Automation and AI assistance exist to support the work, not to replace the role of human judgment.

The human vision-holder remains responsible for:

- direction
- approval
- prioritization
- overrides
- final taste and coherence decisions

---

## Current Role Split

### Human Vision-Holder

The human vision-holder is responsible for:

- setting direction
- defining priorities
- judging quality
- approving or rejecting proposed changes
- deciding when to expand, stabilize, or pause
- protecting the project from drift, overload, and needless bureaucracy

### ChatGPT

ChatGPT currently supports the project by helping with:

- structure
- continuity
- drafting
- synthesis
- narrative planning
- workflow guidance
- distinguishing between what should be done now and what should wait

### Agent

Agent currently supports the project by handling bounded practical tasks such as:

- locating files
- making targeted edits
- creating new scene files
- updating registries
- checking links and scene targets
- reporting concrete findings
- reducing repetitive manual work

---

## Why This Method Exists

The project is too large and too recursive to rely on memory alone.

It also cannot survive if every small act of progress requires tedious manual repetition.

Human-Guided Orchestration exists to create a middle path:

- enough human control to preserve intent
- enough AI assistance to reduce drudgery
- enough automation to keep growth sustainable
- enough play to prevent the work from collapsing into sterile administration

---

## Constraint

Automation should reduce drudgery, not remove judgment.

AI assistance should reduce confusion, not replace direction.

Orchestration should remain clear enough that the project grows through deliberate choices rather than accidental momentum.

If a workflow weakens playability, survivability, or coherence, it should be revised.

---

## Relationship to Narrative High Command

Narrative High Command provides the structural support layer for continuity, registries, and narrative coordination.

Human-Guided Orchestration provides the operational method by which that layer is used.

One concerns narrative command structure.

The other concerns how human judgment, AI reasoning, and automated work are coordinated.

They are related, but not identical.

---

## Working Rule

Build a little.  
Play a little.  
Stabilize a little.  
Expand a little.

Human-Guided Orchestration should preserve that balance.

Too much work and too little play leads to fatigue and burnout.

Too much play and too little structure leads to drift and weak foundations.

The aim is a survivable middle path.

---

## Current Doctrine

The human remains the orchestrator.

AI remains the assistant.

Automation remains the servant of the workflow.

The goal is not maximum automation.

The goal is a growing playable world built through a process that can endure.
